Your supply route

How this appears in real relationships

Dora's page focuses on how Physical touch helps someone feel loved. When this doorway is missed, the hurt often comes from small details that were not named, kept, shared, or safely received.

In a warm sanctuary, she guards closeness after consent and helps the body remember the temperature of safety.

The need underneath

Dora does not guard a demand; this guardian protects a way of receiving care. Use the page to name what needs to be seen, which misfrequency feels tender, and what small action would help.

Practice once through Dora's lens today: name the feeling, name the need in the language of Physical touch, then ask for one small action the other person can do.

How to use the result well

Dora is a doorway in the Heart Garden, not a fixed identity. Use this guardian to describe what helps you feel safe, which misfrequency hurts most, and what small action would make care easier to receive.

Scripts you can use

  • I want to explain this through the language of Physical touch.
  • This is not me making it too big; this is one doorway where I receive love.
  • If you are willing, I would like to start with one small action.

Reflection questions

  1. Was my latest moment of not feeling received connected to Physical touch?
  2. Can Dora's reminder help me shrink the request into one doable step?
  3. Did I state a need as blame instead of a request?
Editorial boundary

The LoveTypes guardians and Heart Garden are metaphor tools for self-reflection and relationship communication. They are not therapy, medical advice, legal advice, or a relationship diagnosis. If you are facing violence, coercive control, trauma, or urgent risk, seek trusted local and professional support first.
